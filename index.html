Taylor Swift

About Taylor Swift

Introduction

Taylor Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. She has a subject of widespread public interest with a vast fanbase, she has influenced the music industry, popular culture, and politics with her wide variety of music.

Early life

Swift had a passion for music at a young age, and she moved fast from playing parts in children's theater to making her stage debut in front of thousands of people. Before a Philadelphia 76ers basketball game, at the age of 11, she sung "The Star-Spangled Banner." The following year, she picked up the guitar and started writing songs. Swift created original music that represented her experiences with tween isolation, drawing influence from country music performers like Shania Twain and the Dixie Chicks (now the Chicks). Swift's parents moved to Hendersonville, Tennessee, when she was 13 years old, giving her more time to pursue country labels in Nashville, which is nearby.
